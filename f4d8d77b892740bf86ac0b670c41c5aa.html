Claims
A claim is a statement made by one identity about another identity or about itself.
Claims are stored on the leaves of a Merkle Tree.
Properties
It is impossible to generate proof of a statement on behalf of an identity without its consent.
Claims can be revoked.
Claims can be updated by creating new versions. When a claim is revoked, no further versions can be made. Claims can be set to be updatable or not with a flag.
Claims can be verified. This means that it is possible to demonstrate cryptographically that a given claim is:
Issued by a specific identity.
Not revoked.
Is of the last version of that claim if it is updatable.
There are two types of claims regarding destination:
Claims about identity's own properties. Example: Operational Key, Ethereum Address, etc.
Claims about another identity's properties:
(Another) Identity has a Property: Directional relation between an identity and a property (See Claim Structure: identity stored in hIndex, i_1).
Property is Owned by (Another) Identity: Directional relation between a property and an identity (See Claim Structure: identity stored in hValue, v_1).
Every identity-based information is represented via a Claim.
A claim is a tuple of the form (holder, type of claim, value), for example: (Bob, age, 101)
Polygon Id follows the usual Holder, Issuer, and Verifier paradigm
Identity Holder, or Prover: An actor who receives a claim. An entity that holds claims in a Wallet
Additionally, the wallet holds the private key of the user and it creates zk-proofs of the claims.
Issuer: An actor who makes a claim.
Verifier: An actor who verifies if the content of a claim is issued by a specific identity and held by another specific identity.
An actor who receives a claim.
Verifies the validity of the zk-proofs held by the Holder.
This always includes at least verifying the signature of the Issuers involved.
Thanks to the zk-ness, the Issuer cannot track an individual credential once it has been issued.
Verification can be done either On-Chain or Off-Chain.
In the On-Chain case, the Verifier is a Smart Contract.
The Holder is the only custodian of its private keys and its claims.
Credential: Data that is needed to prove that a claim is issued by a specific identity and held by another specific identity. This data is composed of a claim and a proof.
Backend: Iden3 and Circom-Iden3
Polygon ID makes use of the projects iden3 and Circom-Iden3 for managing identities with zero knowledge
