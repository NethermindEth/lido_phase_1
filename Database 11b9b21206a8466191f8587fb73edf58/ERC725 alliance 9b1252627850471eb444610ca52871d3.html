AbstractERC 725 is a proposed standard for blockchain-based identity authored by Fabian Vogelsteller, creator of ERC 20 and Web3.js. ERC 725 describes proxy smart contracts that can be controlled by multiple keys and other smart contracts. ERC 735 is an associated standard to add and remove claims to an ERC 725 identity smart contract. These identity smart contracts can describe humans, groups, objects, and machines. ERC 725 lives on the Ethereum blockchain.

Introduction

The ERC-725 smart contract standard manages the identities of people, groups, objects, and machines using multiple keys, each of which serves a specific purpose, including claims by individuals, quorums of keys that act on behalf of an organization's identity, access control, and privileges within an organization. Smart contracts can be accessed through the proxy method in ERC-725, whereas each user must have a contract for herself. The keys are also stored on the expensive EVM storage.

Keys

They propose four purposes for keys:

  • Management keys, which can be used for identity management
  • Action keys, which perform actions associated with this identity (signing, login, transactions, etc.)
  • Claim signer key for signing claims on other identities that must be revokable.
  • Encryption keys, used to encrypt data.

Adding claims

Someone must request a claim from a trustworthy third party to add a claim to their identity. In a message signed by this third party, three items are included: the identity's address, the claim topic, and optionally some supporting data (for example, the hash of the KYC data). This claim would then be stored in the identity contract of the identity owner.

In addition, claims can be self-attested, which is sufficient for a lot of other use cases (email and name, for simple applications without strict KYC requirements, such as a regular news website).

Features of the ERC 725

It consists of two substandards.

ERC725X: It is capable of executing arbitrary smart contracts and deploying other smart contracts.

Functions: Sends a call to another smart contract, transfers the native token to the blockchain, or deploys a new smart contract. Execution function calls must only be made by the current contract owner.

ERC725Y: Holds arbitrary data via generic key-value stores.

Functions: For a single key, set or get data as bytes. Creates an array of data based on multiple keys. Data set for given key is returned.

Features of the ERC 735

To add and remove claims from an ERC 725 identity smart contract, ERC 735 is the associated standard. Claims can be attested by third parties (issuers) or self-attested.

Implementation

The Origin Company worked on the implementation of ERC 725. By connecting your identity to your Ethereum wallet, the DApp allows users to create and control their own identity contracts.

Among the features of Origin's open-source bridge server is an ERC 725 attestation service. It enables them to verify pieces of identity, such as email addresses, phone numbers, and social media accounts, before signing attestations for publication on the blockchain. The process is similar to using a notary service where you trust third parties to verify your and/or others' identities.

They will publish the name, profile picture, and bio to IPFS and store the resulting content hash on the Ethereum blockchain. On the blockchain, the data itself is not stored, just an attestation of their existence. Here is the link of demo implementation and this is the link to the video explaining how it works.

Fabian Vogelsteller is co-founder of LUKSO blockchain who proposed the modular ERC725 smart contract standard.

A LUKSO Standard Proposal (LSP) is the building block of LUKSO's ecosystem, which is implemented with smart contracts. Many LSPs are based on the ERC725 smart contract standard, bringing additional functionality to tokens, NFTs, and the account itself.

LSP0: ERC725 Account

As shown in the figure below, an ERC725Account contains multiple standards.

With the ERC725 Account, users can increase functionality and user-friendliness within the blockchain ecosystem. Additionally, it specifies the relatively basic ERC725 standard by combining expendable smart contract storage and signature verification functionality.

Changing, editing, querying, or removing public profile information is possible. Smart contracts can also be managed from multiple EOAs or other smart contracts with a Key Manager, which means private keys can be exchanged.

By transferring profile control without revealing your private key, it allows for user-friendly backup systems with multiple recovery methods. Moreover, users can manage contracts from a variety of private keys, allowing different brands, creators, and devices to talk as a single DAO or combined identity. Having multiple keys enables collaborative profiles or the management of rights and roles across multiple devices.
